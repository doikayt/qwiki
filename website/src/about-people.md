## Doikayt Vibe

We want to work with people who care about more than a paycheck -- people
who want to build a high-performance engineering culture, where
craftsmanship is taken seriously, and where we turn out software that
delights not just the people who use it, but the people who maintain and
extend it. (See [Compensation Philosophy](/about-company-structure.html#compensation-philosophy)
for how we back that up.)

We reject cancel culture -- the deliberately engineered environment in the
US where everyone is primed to hate each other. We don't police people's
past tweets; we look at where they are now, and whether they're acting in
good faith toward our shared goals, without demanding ideological
lockstep on everything else.

Echoing Frederick Douglass: we will "unite with anybody to do right, and
with nobody to do wrong."

That spirit has real precedent. [Fred Hampton's Rainbow
Coalition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainbow_Coalition_(Fred_Hampton))
built solidarity across racial lines around shared material interests
rather than ideological purity. [Daryl
Davis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daryl_Davis) spent decades
befriending Klan members and talked dozens of them out of the movement.
[Ann Atwater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ann_Atwater), a Durham, NC
civil rights organizer, forged a lasting friendship with a Klan leader
after years of open conflict -- told in Osha Gray Davidson's *The Best of
Enemies: Race and Redemption in the New South*. And [*American History
X*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_History_X) dramatizes the same
arc in fiction: people can leave hatred behind when someone treats them
as capable of change.
