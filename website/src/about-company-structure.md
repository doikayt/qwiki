### Legal

We're now seeking to organize as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. As revenue-generating
work matures, we plan to roll some operational activity into a wholly owned
for-profit subsidiary -- keeping the nonprofit's mission and governance
intact while giving the operational side room to grow sustainably.

### Funding

In our early stage, we're pursuing grants from foundations and
organizations aligned with our mission and specific advocacy positions.
As the for-profit subsidiary matures, we intend to shift toward
self-sustaining revenue from that subsidiary, reducing long-term
reliance on grant funding.

We anticipate two major revenue drivers for the subsidiary:

- **Consulting**, built around our expertise in AI-native software delivery
  and ['lights out' spec-to-code 
workflows](https://medium.com/@clement_86740/the-code-factory-lights-out-coding-in-the-era-of-ai-d1f72fffda70).
- **Application sales** on the Google Workspace Marketplace, leveraging our
  Google Apps Script (GAS) expertise. Specific revenue projections aren't
  published here, but are available on request for prospective funders.


### Compensation Philosophy

This isn't a VC-funded startup chasing a lucrative exit, but we don't
expect our colleagues to work for free. We plan to pay at the low-to-mid
end of the tech labor market, calibrated to whatever region we're hiring
in.

Salaries will be set against public, formula-based bands -- not
manager discretion or negotiating leverage -- drawing on open compensation
data and published pay studies. This isn't a novel idea: companies like
Buffer and GitLab have run fully transparent, formula-driven compensation
for years, on the premise that a public formula is fairer than private
negotiation, and harder to quietly bias.
